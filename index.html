Ticklebot: Low-cost humanoid control platform with integrated AI-ROS2 system for daily task automation

Paper Video Code Data

ROS2–AI Integrated Dual-Arm Control with SmolVLA

- TickleBot employs two low-cost SO-100 open-source robotic arms integrated through ROS2 middleware and the SmolVLA vision-language-action model.
- This enables precise manipulation tasks such as folding and sorting laundry, waste separation, block stacking, and interactive gestures (e.g., high-fives) with enhanced accessibility and reduced hardware cost.

NoMaD-Based Autonomous Navigation and Task Execution

- The mobile base of TickleBot leverages the NoMaD model to achieve vision-based navigation using only RGB cameras.
- By connecting the mobile base with the dual-arm system through ROS2 nodes, the robot can autonomously travel to a target location and perform manipulation tasks on-site, enabling household-level generalization of robotic actions at low cost.

Abstract

This project introduces TickleBot, a low-cost open-source robotic research platform that lowers the entry barrier to humanoid robotics for students and researchers.

Recent advances in Physical AI have highlighted the importance of foundation models for robotics, yet the absence of standardized, accessible hardware platforms and the high cost of commercial robotic arms continue to hinder progress and scalability. In this work, we present TickleBot, a low-cost, open-source robotic research platform designed to lower the entry barrier for undergraduate students and researchers. Built at approximately USD 725, TickleBot integrates the SO-100 open-source robotic arm with a ROS2-based middleware, enabling precise manipulation and mobility without requiring a physical twin. By incorporating the NoMaD model for mobile base navigation and SmolVLA for upper-body control, the platform supports AI-driven teleoperation and task execution using only RGB camera inputs.

TickleBot is designed not only as a research tool but also as an educational resource. Its affordability and modularity make it suitable for integration into university curricula, providing students with hands-on experience in robotics and AI integration. Furthermore, as an open-source platform, TickleBot encourages community-driven development and rapid dissemination of robotic technologies. Beyond education and research, its extensibility allows potential applications in manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and assistive services. Through TickleBot, we aim to democratize access to humanoid robotics and foster innovation by bridging the gap between advanced AI models and practical, low-cost robotic systems.

Laundry Folding

The robot arm recognizes a T-shirt placed on the table using its vision system, grasps it with the gripper, and executes a folding sequence. After folding, the arm neatly places the garment in an organized stack.

Waste Sorting

Using object recognition, the robot identifies different waste items such as cans and paper cups. It then classifies the objects by type and places each one into the corresponding recycling bin using precise arm movements.
